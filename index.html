His wind-swirled fingertips untangle the roots of salt water, thorned with wrens
pecking at roans whipping their manes toward some new sunlight, some new charred horse bit’s history.
Cornered into being a son he should have never left a snow-tipped leaf’s edge
in the hillside where a barren cloud’s porous skull keeps a winter house.
Coyote, open-jawed, limp shoulder against his ear, its silhouette: midnight blue a satchel of stars where its tail snaps awake
